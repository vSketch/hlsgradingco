Frequently Asked Questions

Grading is the process of leveling or sloping the ground to prepare it for construction. This can include flattening areas for foundations or roads or creating slopes for drainage systems.

Excavation involves removing soil, rock, or other materials to create trenches, foundations, or holes for structures like buildings, pipes, or utilities. It’s a key step in many construction projects.

If you're planning a construction project, such as building a home or installing a driveway, or if you’re experiencing drainage issues, your property may require grading. A professional can evaluate the slope and terrain to determine what’s necessary.

Proper grading ensures good drainage, preventing water pooling and erosion, which can damage structures. It also provides a stable foundation for any construction, from homes to roads.

The length of a grading project depends on the size of the property, the complexity of the terrain, and weather conditions. Most residential projects can take anywhere from a few days to a couple of weeks.

Grading and excavation can involve heavy equipment, but our team takes care to minimize disruptions to your property. We also make sure to restore the area once the work is complete.
